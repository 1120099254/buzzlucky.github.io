Lesson Recap:

As repeated from the previous lesson:

When you have more then ‘one’ of something, it is considered a ‘plural’.

So, to put it simply:

1 = singular

2+ = plural

Regarding Is & Are…

is = singular

are = plural

So,

We use ‘is’ when talking about something ‘singular’ (1).

We use ‘are’ when talking about something ‘plural’ (2+).


Examples:


This is

a flower (1)

a car (1)

a boy (1)

The boy is swimming. (1 boy)

The cat is running. (1 cat)

She is sleeping. (1 girl)


These are

dogs (2+)

cups (2+)

books (2+)

The boys are swimming. (2+ boys)

The cats are running. (2+ cats)

They are sleeping. (2+ people)
