Tangadan falls is one of the most sought-after waterfalls in La Union. Yet getting here was not a walk in the park: From San Gabriel's municipal hall, one must ride a "habal-habal"
(motorcycle) or a monster jeepney that goes through the winding roads for quite a few kilometers. After the wearying ride comes another trek that lasts about thirty minutes. Nevertheless, once you hear the rushing water, all the struggles will just melt away, for a marvelous sight will be in front of your eyes.

Tangadan is a two-tier cascade along Baroro River with cold water emerging from the wilderness of the province alongside the Cordillera Mountain Range. It is a noteworthy cliff diving site (towering around 50 feet) amongst the adventurous souls, an ideal site for nature enthusiasts and an idyllic spot for surfers who wants to take a break from chasing the waves.

Special thanks to our local guide Mr. Reymond Nebres. He is an accredited tour/eco guide of Tangadan Falls certified by Department of Tourism.
