Among the most important tips and points that you should pay attention to when writing a CV are:
Keep it short, simple, and convenient. Anything longer than three pages will automatically go to the trash. Just tell the recruiter what they need to know. Be sure to write it clearly - and to not be misspelled! It is no frills ... or photographs. The hiring professional or human resource manager doesn't want to know what people look like, they just want to know what they've done and what they can do.


As for the education that might be appropriate to be mentioned in the CV?
Maybe your high school, but it's mostly not important
The most important thing is additional education such as a university or college, then any professional qualifications you may have, in addition to work experience, of course, my understanding is very important.
