Widespread soil erosion, created by mass deforestation and poor farming practices throughout the basin, is silting up hydropower stations and threatening to cause long-term damage to water supplies, food security and the region’s micro-climate. Reversing the decline is in the hands of smallholder farmers and their communities but finding the right incentive to encourage the required change takes a novel approach.
