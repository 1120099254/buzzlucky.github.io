--- title: "It is important 'not to inflame the situation any further' with China" image: "https:\/\/i.ytimg.com\/vi\/j49_5z5M0_o\/hqdefault.jpg" vid_id: "j49_5z5M0_o" categories: "News-Politics" tags: ["viewpoint","world","msn"] date: "2020-12-05T22:27:40+03:00" vid_date: "2020-12-01T03:04:15Z" duration: "PT5M33S" viewcount: "33880" likeCount: "253" dislikeCount: "408" channel: "Sky News Australia" --- It is very important that Prime Minister Scott Morrison must now "take the passion out of this" after a Chinese official posted a doctored image of an Australian soldier on Twitter, according to ANU Professor John Blaxland.

On Monday, Prime Minister Scott Morrison demanded China apologise for posting the doctored image of an Australian soldier holding a knife to the throat of a child.

Mr Morrison had also demanded Twitter remove the violent, fake image but the social media giant has failed to act.

Discussing Mr Morrison's response, Professor Blaxland said, "on one level, yes it does appear to be the right move".

"But it's very important now that the prime minister take the passion out of this," he told Sky News.

"And if I can use a cricket analogy, play with a straight bat".

"It's very important that we don't inflame the situation any further".
